Meditation Guide
1. Find a Comfortable Position

Sit in a chair with your feet flat on the floor, or on a cushion on the floor. Keep your back straight but not stiff.

2. Set Your Intention

Take a moment to remind yourself why you're meditating today.

3. Focus on Your Breath

Feel the sensation of your breath as it enters and leaves your body. Notice the rise and fall of your chest and abdomen.

4. Notice When Your Mind Wanders

When you notice your attention has drifted, gently redirect it back to your breath without judging yourself.

5. Be Kind to Yourself

If you find yourself getting frustrated, remember that noticing your mind has wandered is a moment of mindfulness itself.

6. End Gently

When the timer ends, take a moment to notice how you feel before slowly opening your eyes and moving your body.

Benefits of Regular Meditation
Reduces Stress

Regular meditation can significantly lower stress hormone levels.

Improves Emotional Health

Creates greater self-awareness and helps manage difficult emotions.

Enhances Focus

Strengthens attention and concentration abilities.

Improves Sleep

Helps calm the mind and prepare the body for restful sleep.
