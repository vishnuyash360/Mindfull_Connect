What is Mindfulness?

Mindfulness is the practice of purposefully focusing your attention on the present moment—and accepting it without judgment. It's about being fully engaged with whatever you're doing, free from distraction or judgment, and aware of your thoughts and feelings without getting caught up in them.

Research has shown that mindfulness practices can reduce stress, anxiety, and depression while improving focus, emotional regulation, and overall wellbeing.

Getting Started with Mindfulness

Tips for Beginners
Start Small

Begin with just 5 minutes of practice daily. It's better to do a short session consistently than longer sessions sporadically.

Be Patient and Kind to Yourself

Mind-wandering is normal. When you notice your attention has drifted, gently bring it back without self-criticism.

Find a Comfortable Position

You don't need to sit cross-legged. Any position that's comfortable and allows you to stay alert works.

Incorporate Mindfulness Into Daily Activities

Practice while eating, walking, or showering by fully engaging with the sensory experience.

Common Challenges

The goal isn't to stop thinking but to notice thoughts without getting caught up in them. Think of thoughts as clouds passing in the sky—acknowledge them, then let them drift away.

Even 5 minutes daily can be beneficial. You can also practice informal mindfulness during everyday activities like brushing your teeth, walking, or waiting in line.

This is normal, especially for beginners. Try a different position or a walking meditation instead. Sometimes the discomfort itself can become an object of mindful attention.

If you're noticing your experience—whether it's your breath, bodily sensations, or the fact that your mind is wandering—you're practicing mindfulness. There's no perfect way to do it.
