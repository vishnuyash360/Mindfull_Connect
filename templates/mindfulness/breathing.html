About Breathing Exercises

Breathing exercises are powerful tools for managing stress and anxiety. By controlling your breath, you can activate your parasympathetic nervous system (rest and digest mode) and reduce the activity of your sympathetic nervous system (fight or flight mode).

Regular practice of breathing exercises can help:

  • Reduce stress and anxiety
  • Lower blood pressure
  • Improve focus and concentration
  • Promote better sleep
  • Increase energy levels

Try to practice these exercises daily for the best results.

Tips for Effective Practice
Find a Comfortable Position

Sit comfortably with your back straight, or lie down if preferred.

Minimize Distractions

Choose a quiet place without interruptions for your practice.

Breathe Through Your Nose

Nasal breathing filters and warms the air, and activates the diaphragm better.

Start Small

Begin with just 2-3 minutes of practice and gradually increase the duration.
