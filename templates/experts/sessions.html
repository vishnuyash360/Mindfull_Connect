Tips for Successful Q&A Sessions
Choosing a Topic

Focus on specific areas of mental health rather than broad topics. This helps attract participants who are genuinely interested in that subject.

Session Description

Be clear about what you'll cover and what participants can expect to learn. Setting expectations helps ensure a more productive session.

Timing Considerations

Consider scheduling during evenings or weekends when more community members are likely to be available. Avoid very early or late hours.

Duration

30-60 minutes is usually optimal. Shorter sessions may feel rushed, while longer ones can lead to fatigue.

Participant Limits

Smaller groups (10-20 participants) often allow for more meaningful interaction and give everyone a chance to participate.
