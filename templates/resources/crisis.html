Warning Signs

Be aware of these warning signs that someone may be in crisis:

  • Talking about wanting to die or kill oneself
  • Looking for ways to kill oneself
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or having no purpose
  • Talking about feeling trapped or being in unbearable pain
  • Talking about being a burden to others
  • Increasing use of alcohol or drugs
  • Acting anxious, agitated, or reckless
  • Sleeping too little or too much
  • Withdrawing or feeling isolated
  • Showing rage or talking about seeking revenge
  • Displaying extreme mood swings

How to Help Others

If you're concerned about someone:

  1. Ask directly if they are thinking about suicide
  2. Listen without judgment
  3. Keep them safe - remove means of self-harm if possible
  4. Stay with them
  5. Help them connect with support resources
  6. Follow up afterward
Remember: Taking action can save a life. Don't keep suicidal thoughts a secret.
