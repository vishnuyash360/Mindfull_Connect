Problems I found before hosting
1. You uploaded your virtual environment

Your zip contains:

.venv/

This should never be uploaded to GitHub or a hosting service.

Delete:

.venv/

before uploading.

2. Your requirements.txt is broken

Your requirements.txt is UTF-16 encoded.

You'll notice it looks like

b l i n k e r = = 1 . 9 . 0 

Most hosting providers will fail reading this.

Convert it to normal UTF-8.

3. Your .env contains passwords

Your project currently contains

DATABASE_URL=postgresql://postgres:1234...

and

PGPASSWORD=0000

Never upload these to GitHub.

Instead, add them as Environment Variables on the hosting platform.

Possible deployment issues

I noticed these packages

psycopg2
nltk

Sometimes Render fails compiling psycopg2.

Replace

psycopg2

with

psycopg2-binary

in requirements.txt.
